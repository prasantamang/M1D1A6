Common elements


The various code of football share certain common elements and can be grouped into two main classes of football:carring codes like American football,
Canadian football, Australian football, rugby union and ruby league, where the ball is moved about the field while being held in the hands or thrown, and
kicking codes such as Association football and Gaelic football, where the ball is moved primarity with the feet, and where handling is strictly limitted.[11]


Common rules among the sports include.[12]
  • Two teams of usually between 11 and 18 players; some variations that have fewer players(five or more per team) are also popular.
  • A clearly defiened area in which to play the game.
  • scoring goals or points by moving the ball to an opposite teams's end of the field and either into a goal area, or over a line.
  • Goals or points resulting from players putting the ball between two goalposts.
  • The goals or line being defended by the opposing team.
  • players using only their body to move the ball.
    In all codes, common skills include passing, tackling, evansion of tackles, catching and kicking. [11] In most codes, there are rules restricting the movement of
    players offside, and players scoring a goal must put the ball either under or over a crossbar between the goalposts.
